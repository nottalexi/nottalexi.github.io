Neuroepigenomics Lab

Research vision

Genetic variation located outside of genes has been associated with an increased risk of aging-related brain disorders such as Alzheimer’s disease. These genetic ‘risk’ variants may regulate gene expression from afar through a phenomenon termed epigenetics. However, the target genes and affected brain cell types of these disease-risk variants are unclear. Our group is addressing this challenge by understanding how the epigenome and chromatin architecture regulates cell type gene expression in aging-related brain disorders. We are approaching these research areas using next-generation sequencing of patient-derived brain tissue and stem cell-derived models of disease.

The lab is located in the UK Dementia Research Institute (DRI) and the Department of Brain Sciences at Imperial College.
